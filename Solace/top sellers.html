THE WORLD IS AT YOUR FEET, SO MAKE SURE YOU ARE WEARING GREAT SHOES.

1) Air Jordan 1 Retro

The Air Jordan 1 High debuted in 1985 as the first signature sneaker developed by Nike for Michael Jordan. The Peter Moore designed performance basketball sneaker featured a simple Nike Dunk inspired design that incorporated the Nike Swoosh and the Jordan Wings logo and featured Nike Air.

2) Converse Chuck Taylor All Stars

Converse All Star shoe was designed after the company made improvements to the design based on Chuck Taylor's input. In addition, Taylor's signature was incorporated into the high-top's ankle patch, resulting in the design that became known as the Chuck Taylor All Star.

3) Air Force 1

Air Force 1 was designed by Bruce Kilgore and introduced in 1982, It was the first ever basketball shoe to feature Nike Air technology, revolutionizing the game and sneaker culture forever.

4) Air jordan 11

Air Jordan 11 debuted in 1995 as Michael Jordan's 11th signature sneaker. Designed by Tinker Hatfield, the Jordan 11 was the first Air Jordan to feature patent leather and carbon fiber in addition to cordura nylon.

5) Nike Air Penny 2

Nike Air Penny 2 was designed by Eric Avar and originally released in 1997. They were retroed for the first time in 2008. The sneaker features reflective 3M detailing, a visible Air Sole unit in the heel, Phylon midsole and rubber outsole.

6) Air jordan 12

The Air Jordan XII features gold-plated steel lace loops, embossed lizard skin pattern and a full length zoom air unit with a carbon fiber shank plate, this model set a new direction in style and technology in shoe design.
